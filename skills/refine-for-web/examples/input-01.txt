BSc Environmental Science

The Department of Environmental Science at the University offers a comprehensive programme covering all aspects of the natural environment. Students will gain a thorough grounding in core scientific disciplines including ecology, geology, atmospheric science and hydrology, as well as developing strong quantitative and analytical skills.

The programme has been running for over 25 years and enjoys an excellent reputation within the sector. We have strong links with industry and government bodies including the Environment Agency, Natural England and several leading environmental consultancies, which provides students with excellent opportunities for placements and graduate employment.

Our staff are active researchers and bring their work directly into teaching. Professor Jane Walsh leads our climate adaptation research group, which has received significant UKRI funding. Students in their final year may have the opportunity to contribute to live research projects.

The degree is accredited by the Institution of Environmental Sciences (IES).

Teaching takes place through a combination of lectures, seminars, laboratory practicals and fieldwork. We run residential field courses in the UK and internationally — recent destinations have included Iceland and the Cairngorms. In year 2, students undertake a 6-week placement with an external organisation.

Students who graduate from this programme go on to careers in environmental consultancy, conservation, policy, research and education. Our graduates have gone on to work for organisations including Arup, WWF, DEFRA and various local authorities. Graduate employability is 94% within 6 months (2022/23 Graduate Outcomes survey).

Entry requirements:
- A levels: AAB including Biology or Geography (Chemistry, Environmental Science, or Geology also accepted as the science subject)
- BTEC: DDD in a relevant subject
- Scottish Highers: AAABB including Biology or Geography
- International Baccalaureate: 34 points overall, with 6,5,5 at Higher Level including Biology or Geography
- We welcome applications from mature students and those with non-standard qualifications — contact the admissions team to discuss

Fees (2025/26):
- UK students: £9,535 per year
- International students: £19,800 per year

There is a compulsory field course fee of approximately £400 in Year 1 and £600 in Year 2. Bursaries are available for students who would face financial hardship — contact the student finance team for details.
